Introduction to Halal Food in Northwest China

The halal food in Northwest China is rich in variety, with strong local characteristics and unique cooking techniques. Since Northwest China is located inland, the natural landscape is mostly plateaus, grasslands and Gobi. Most of the food here uses high-quality ingredients and is carefully cooked to retain the original flavor of the ingredients. At the same time, Northwest China is a region where many ethnic groups live together,Especially many Muslims live in this area so these foods also carry a rich cultural background. Northwest China's halal food can be roughly divided into three categories according to the ingredients and cooking techniques: Xinjiang halal food, Gansu halal food and Shaanxi halal food. Halal Food from Xinjiang

  • XinJiang Lagman: A hand-pulled noodle dish served with vegetables and lamb, cooked in a spicy sauce.
  • Roast Lamb: Tender and juicy lamb, seasoned with cumin, chili, and other spices, roasted to perfection.
  • Pilaf (Polu): A hearty rice dish cooked with lamb, carrots, onions, and a variety of spices.

Halal Food from Gansu

  • "Dong Xiang" Hand-Pulled Lamb:Hand-Pulled Lamb is a medicinal food made with lamb loin meat and coriander as the main ingredients.
  • Lanzhou Beef Noodles:Famous for its delicious beef soup base, hand-pulled noodles and rich ingredients.
  • San Paotai:Made with Gansu Linze jujube, Fujian longan, Xinjiang raisins, dried lychees and high-quality rock sugar as condiments.

Halal Food from Shaanxi

  • Roujiamo: Often called a “Chinese hamburger,” it is a bun filled with spiced, shredded lamb or beef.
  • Yangrou Paomo: A traditional lamb stew served with crumbled flatbread.
  • Cold Noodles: Noodles served cold with a spicy, garlicky sauce, often topped with cucumber and chili oil.
